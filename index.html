The group works on energy for irrigation, renewable energy, and rural electricity supply. The research covers end-use, techno-economics, and policy in the sector. We use a systemic lens to analyze the implementation and use of technology with the overarching goal of equitable and sustainable development.

An equally important parallel component is an integration of engineering curriculum with development problems. The undergriding philosophy is that orienting education to local problems of societal importance are more motivating for students, result in better learning outcomes due to familiarity of the field situations, and also feed back for better development outcomes.
